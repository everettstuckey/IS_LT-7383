Solid

Solids have a stable definite shape and volume. Solids only change shape by force, and they transform to liquids by melting.

Liquid

Liquids take the shape of their container and have a definite volume under certain conditions. Solids may become liquid through melting.

Gas

Gases conform and expand to fill their container. They have no definite shape or volume, and liquids may become gas under certain conditions.

Plasma

Plasmas do not have a definite shape or volume and have unique properties such as electrical conductivity and responsiveness to electromagnetic forces.
